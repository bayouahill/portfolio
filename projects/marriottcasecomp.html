One Marriott: Cultivating Sustainable Changes

Project Overview

The One Marriott case competition addressed critical student support and engagement issues at BYU Marriott School of Business. Through comprehensive survey research of 400 pre-business students, the project identified significant gaps in student satisfaction (only 38% felt supported), gender disparity in enrollment (36% women vs 54% national average), and resource accessibility. The analysis informed strategic recommendations to cultivate a unified school culture.

Key Features

  • Multi-phase survey methodology with 400+ pre-business student respondents
  • Comparative analysis of BYU enrollment data against national averages
  • A/B testing of communication strategies measuring engagement impact
  • Data visualization of student support metrics and resource awareness
  • Strategic recommendations across three pillars: identity cultivation, brand growth, and survey reform

Technical Approach

Survey design focused on measuring student perceptions of support, intimidation factors, and resource accessibility. Analysis included statistical comparison of gender enrollment trends, engagement metrics from communication experiments, and qualitative assessment of existing institutional structures. Results were synthesized into data-driven recommendations emphasizing clear objectives, concise survey design, and alignment with institutional mission. Presentation delivered via Canva with supporting visualizations and comparative data charts.
